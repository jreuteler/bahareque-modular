# Environmental Impact

## Carbon Footprint Per Panel — Baseline Mix

Figures below reflect the current baseline spec: L 40×40×4 steel frame + 30 % guadua cavity content + cement-sand mortar (baseline) + 10 mm lime plaster both faces. See [Panel Anatomy](02-panel-anatomy.md) and [Materials](03-materials.md) for details.

| Material | Qty per panel | Embodied CO₂ (kg) | Source of emission |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Portland cement (~18 % of mortar mass) | ~54 kg | 43.2 | Calcination + kiln fuel (~0.8 kg CO₂/kg cement) |
| Steel L-angle frame + clamping strips | ~22 kg steel | 39.6 | Steelmaking (~1.8 kg CO₂/kg steel) |
| Galvanizing (zinc) | ~0.7 kg zinc | 2.8 | Zinc smelting (~4 kg CO₂/kg zinc) |
| Sand (~82 % of mortar mass) | ~240 kg | 1.2 | Extraction + transport |
| Chicken wire + aluminum mesh | ~2 kg | 3.6 | Steel wire production |

| Cables + connectors | ~0.5 kg | 1.5 | Copper + plastic |
| HDPE blocks | ~0.5 kg | 0.5 | If recycled: ~1 kg CO₂/kg (vs 3 for virgin) |

## Carbon Footprint Per Panel — Optimized Bahareque Mix

The optimized mix (pozzolanic lime + pasto estrella + 30 % guadua + 5 mm plaster) delivers lower embodied carbon through multiple levers:

| Lever | CO₂ delta per panel vs baseline |
|---|---|
| Replace 30 % cement with fly ash or RHA pozzolan | −10 to −13 kg |
| Replace Portland cement mortar with hydraulic lime base + pozzolan | −8 to −12 kg |
| Replace PP fiber with pasto estrella (star grass) | −0.5 kg |
| Thinner 5 mm lime plaster both faces (−50 % lime volume) | −9 kg calcination, +4 kg reduced carbonation recovery = **−5 kg net** |
| **Total reduction** | **−24 to −30 kg CO₂ per panel** |
| **Net CO₂ per panel, optimized mix** | **~6 to 20 kg CO₂** |
| **Net CO₂ per m² of wall, optimized** | **~3 to 8 kg CO₂/m²** |

With locally available pozzolans (volcanic ash in the Colombian coffee region, rice husk ash from Tolima/Valle del Cauca) and pasto estrella fiber from the property itself, the optimized bahareque mix approaches **carbon-neutral** per panel. If the bamboo stand is managed as a managed carbon sink (coppice rotation with biomass permanence), the system becomes **carbon-negative**: the wall absorbs more CO₂ during its life than was emitted to build it.

| Concrete block (15 cm, plastered) | 40–60 | 3–4× baseline |

| Reinforced concrete wall (15 cm) | 70–100 | 5–7× baseline |

The panel system's baseline mix produces **~3× less CO₂ than concrete block or brick** — the two most common wall materials in the developing world. The optimized bahareque mix approaches traditional earth construction levels while providing vastly superior structural performance and lifespan.

## Path to Carbon-Neutral or Carbon-Negative

The panel system is already low-carbon at baseline. With the optimized bahareque mix and the following further substitutions, it goes **carbon-negative**:

| Substitution | CO₂ saved per panel | Feasibility |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Use pozzolanic lime mortar (optimized mix) | −20 to −25 kg | Core spec — see above |
| Use recycled steel (electric arc furnace) | −8 to −12 kg | Depends on local steel supply chain |
| Increase bamboo content further (45 % cavity) | −5 to −8 kg additional sequestration | Material is free/cheap |
| Use bio-lime (wood-fired kiln with coppiced fuel) | −5 kg | Traditional lime production method |
| Thinner plaster (5 mm → 3 mm) | −5 kg | Traditional bahareque practice |
| **Total potential reduction beyond optimized** | **−43 to −55 kg** | |

At maximum reduction, net CO₂ per panel drops into the **−5 to +5 kg** range — carbon-neutral to slightly negative. If the bamboo stand is managed as a permanent carbon sink, the system is reliably **carbon-negative over its service life**.

## Water Use

- **Mortar mixing:** ~15–20 liters per panel (curing water reused where possible)
- **Curing:** ~50 liters over 7 days (misting/burlap method)
- **Lime plaster mixing:** ~10 liters per panel
- **Total:** ~75–80 liters per panel, or ~30 liters per m² of wall

For comparison: concrete block production uses ~40–50 liters per m² of wall. Fired brick production uses ~100+ liters per m² (including kiln cooling).

## Waste

- **Steel offcuts:** Recycled (scrap value)
- **Bamboo offcuts:** Composted or used as garden stakes/mulch
- **Mortar waste:** Minimal — vibration table pour is precise. Any excess is reused in the next panel.
- **Sand bed:** Reused indefinitely (sand is not consumed, just compressed and loosened)
- **HDPE offcuts:** Recycled or reused for smaller blocks
- **Packaging waste:** Minimal — most materials arrive in bulk

The system produces **near-zero construction waste**. This is a direct consequence of workshop prefabrication — every cut is planned, every material is measured.

## End of Life (after 80–100 years)

When a panel eventually reaches end of life:

| Material | Disposal | Environmental impact |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| Steel frame | Recycle (100% recyclable) | Positive — scrap steel has value |
| Mortar + bamboo | Crush and use as aggregate or fill | Inert — mortar is calcium/silica/sand, bamboo is organic |
| Lime plaster | Returns to calcium carbonate (limestone) | Carbon-neutral — CO₂ absorbed during carbonation is re-released, net zero |
| HDPE blocks | Recycle | Recyclable |
| Copper cables | Recycle (high scrap value) | Positive |
| PVC conduit | Recycle where facilities exist | Recyclable in principle |

**No hazardous waste.** No asbestos, no lead paint, no synthetic insulation foam, no pressure-treated wood chemicals. The panel is made of steel, bamboo, sand, cement, and lime — it returns to the earth or the recycling stream cleanly.
